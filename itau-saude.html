Itaú Saúde

problem

This project constitutes of redesigning the site and app of Itaú Saúde, the health insurance of Itaú (the biggest Brazilian bank) employees. Before the project, the app and site were offered by the insurance company Porto Seguro, and they didn't match with the online presence of the bank.

users

The users were employees and ex-employees of Itaú.

team and
role

This project was done inside Ana Couto Design. I was responsible for making the wireframe, information architecture, the UI of some crucial pages (for the UI team create news interfaces based on that), and some screens of the app.

challenges

One of our most significant challenges in this project was to design with elderly adults in mind.

process

After the diagnosis made by the team of Ana Couto, we concluded that we have to design a site that is inclusive for aged people. So we need:
- To built a website with excellent legibility and the right size of typography and contrast (ref. ↗)
- An information architecture that was easy for the user to locate was he need.

Information architecture

We made a benchmark with the biggest insurance companies in Brazil and other sites that we considered inclusive for elderly users. One crucial reference was gov.uk.

Gov.uk

how we tackle the challenge

We made almost all esthetical decisions based on our users,  specifically the elderly ones. We chose a big and contrast typography and made the buttons similar to the default hyperlink's look. In that way, maintaining a well-know pattern. We worked a lot on the architecture information and decided to put on the homepage quick links to the most useful features. We also used smalls links to other features and all the documents the user could need. He could find anything through the menu, the search, or scanning the homepage. Not demanding a specific way to navigate between pages.

TL;DR

Itaú (the biggest bank in Brazil) hired us to redesign the portal and app of the health insurance that they offer for its employees. We designed primarily with elderly users in mind. Focusing on legibility, contrast, maintaining well-known patterns, and easy navigation.
